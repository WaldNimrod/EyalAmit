🔴 THE FINDING THAT MATTERS — §4: the catch-all was silently dropping real content
  The mechanism ran `#^/Blog/(.+)$# -> /blog/$1` BEFORE the exact $map and exit()ed, so an exact
  /Blog/<slug> decision was UNREACHABLE — the catch-all always won. A prefix rewrite cannot follow
  a RENAME, so any post whose slug changed between the old and new site got 301'd into a 404.
  The full triage found 4 such posts. All are live; all were unreachable from their legacy URL:
     /Blog/הכל-אודות-ריברסינג-נשימה-מעגלית-ו-דיג/   -> /ריברסינג-נשימה-מעגלית-דיגרידו/
     /Blog/נשים-מנגנות-בדיגרידו-אישה-מנגנת-בדיג-2/  -> /נשים-מנגנות-בדיגרידו-אישה-מנגנת-בדיג/
     /Blog/סדנה-קבוצתית-מקיפה-וייחודית-לנשימה-מע/   -> /סדנת-דיגרידו-קבוצתית-מקיפה-וייחודית-ל/
     /Blog/את-הספר-החדש-שלי-לא-תמצאו-בחנויות-הספרי/ -> /את-הספר-החדש-שלי-לא-תמצאו-ברשתות-הספרים/
  Each verified by TITLE match against the live post, not by slug similarity alone.
  FIX (in the GENERATOR, never the artifact): $map is now emitted BEFORE the regex and the regex
  yields to it — `if ( ! isset( $map[$norm] ) && preg_match(...) )`. An exact decision beats the
  catch-all. Verified live: all 4 now 301 single-hop -> 200; un-decided slugs still ride the
  catch-all (2 hops -> 200). No regression.
